Japanese Cuisine

Japanese cuisine focuses on bringing out the most savory flavors from the various ingredients. With a focus on harmonizing the various ingredients of the seasons, Japanese cooking reminds one of a quiet life on a small farm or a fishing village. A traditional meal consists of rice with miso soup, some pickled vegetables, and another side dish (Okazu). The Okazu is usually some form of seafood, and can be grilled or raw. Other specialities include tempura, vegetables or seafood deepfried in a light batter. Japanese cuisine is extremely adept at adopting foreign cuisines, and has many foods that originated in China and other parts of Asia. With modernization, Japan has adopted many western foods such as hamburgers and steaks, but still maintain their distinct flavors.
